Frequently Asked Questions

Your questions answered about physiotherapy and injuries.

1. What is physiotherapy?

Physiotherapy is a healthcare profession that helps individuals improve their physical function and mobility through various techniques, including exercise, manual therapy, and education.

2. What types of injuries can physiotherapy help with?

Physiotherapy can help with a wide range of injuries, including sports injuries, post-surgical rehabilitation, chronic pain conditions, and musculoskeletal disorders.

3. How long does a physiotherapy session last?

A typical physiotherapy session lasts between 30 to 60 minutes, depending on the treatment plan and the specific needs of the patient.

4. Do I need a referral to see a physiotherapist?

In many cases, you do not need a referral to see a physiotherapist. However, some insurance plans may require a referral for coverage, so it's best to check with your provider.

5. What should I wear to my physiotherapy appointment?

Wear comfortable clothing that allows for easy movement. Depending on your treatment, you may need to change into shorts or a tank top.

6. How many sessions will I need?

The number of sessions required varies based on the individual and the nature of the injury. Your physiotherapist will create a personalized treatment plan and discuss the expected duration of therapy.
